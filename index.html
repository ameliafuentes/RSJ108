How To Reduce Your Carbon Footprint

Assembly time: everyday!
Awesome rating: 1000%

Everyday is Earth Day! No one person can save the earth by themselves, but if we all make a concious effort to lessen our impact we sure can get one step closer! Want to learn just a few ways you can begin saving our planet? Follow some of the sustainable steps below!

  • How To Reduce Your Carbon Footprint

  • Reduce, Reuse, Recycle
  • On Your Plate
  • Sustainable Fashion
  • Drive Less, Save More
  • Turn It OFF (in your home)
  • Stay Informed And Spread The Word!

  1. Reduce, Reuse, Recycle

    this step can be put into practice multiple ways! here are just a few:

    1. Skip the plastic and use reusable bags, water bottles, utensils, etc.
    2. Go paperless. Opt in for digital receipts and bills. Did you know the average person recieves about 30 pounds of junk mail a year?
    3. Start recycling! Buy yourself a recycling bin and make a concious effort to use it

  2. What you eat matters

    Globally, 60% of emissions are linked to what we put on our plate. Farming animals for meat and dairy requires massive space and huge inputs of water and feed. Moving away from a meat and dairy dominated diet towards a plant-based diet can lower your impact on emissions by a lot!

    The beef and dairy industry is one of the biggest contributors to global greenhouse gases

  3. Dressing Sustainably

    You Don't Have To Give Up Your Glamour To Be Sustainable

  4. On The Road In The Sky

    In 2017 carbon dioxide emissions from transportation surpassed emissions from electricity generation. Going carless could save about 2.6 tons of carbon dioxide.

    Here Are Some Alternative Modes Of Trasportation:

    1. Ride a bike
    2. Take the bus or train
    3. Carpool with friends or coworkers
    4. Get some excercise and walk!

  5. Unplug when you're not at home

    Changes to our use of heat, light and appliances can make some of the biggest differences.

    1. Turn down the heat. Keeping your blinds closed helps keep the temperature inside stable
    2. Take shorter showers. Save water and shower with a friend!
    3. Replace your lights. Did you know LED lights use up to 85% less energy and last 25 times longer than incandescent lights?
    4. unplug any unnecessary devices that aren't in use whether you're at home or away

  6. Encouraging Sustainability

    Climate change can be an overwhelming concept to wrap around your head. The science is complex and when it comes to future impacts, there are still a lot of unknowns.

    While real solutions require global efforts, there are still day-to-day choices you can make to lessen your personal impact.

    Remember, we only have one earth. Take care of her!
